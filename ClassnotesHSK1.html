My Class Notes For Chinese Language HSK1

What The Notes Are For

This is simply my collection of what I have been attempting to learn of Mandarin Chinese through the year 2020, both during brief attendances at the classroom at Bendigo Senior Secondary College, 40 Gaol Rd, Bendigo, VIC.

I have formatted the material to suit my own learning style and have published this material in the hope that it may be useful for others.

Should this material be found wanting, or otherwise objectionable to the Mandarin teaching authorities at BSSC I will simply take the site down on request.

What The Notes Are Not.

This material is in no way a course in Mandarin. To learn the language a student really must be guided by a native, qualified language speaker. However, given that the reader is undergoing such instruction, the material I present may be a useful adjunct to the efforts of your native speakers, especially for revision, that being my purpose in writing it.

Pronunciation Exercises 3

The Tone Sandhi of "不 (bù)"

When "不" is followed by a syllable in the first, second or third tone, its tone doesn't change.

不吃bù chīTo not eat 不行bù xíngnot OK
不好bù hǎonot good 不喝Bù hēto not drink
不能/td>bù néngcan't 不想bù xiǎngdon't want

When "不" is followed by a syllable in the fourth tone, it changes into the second tone.

不会bú huìto be unable to 不是bú shìto be not
不看bú kànto not look

NB The pinyin in the second table shows bú in the second tone. This is artificial, it is normally always shown as fourth tone, but should always be pronounced as second tone when followed by a syllable of the fourth tone.

Pronunciation Exercises 4

Rules of Pinyin (2): ü or finals led by ü with j, q, x

When ü is preceded by j, q, or x, the two dots over ü are removed but not so when ü is preceded by l or n. This is because lu and nu are allowed constructions in Pinyin as well as lü and nü.

üüeüanün
jujuejuanjun
ququequanqun
xuxuexuanxun

Pronunciation Exercises 3

Tone Sandhi of "一 yī "

When "一" is followed by a syllable in the first, second, or third tone, it changes into the fourth tone.

  • 一张 yì zhāng one sheet/piece

  • 一条 yì tiáo one (long) piece

  • 一种 yì zhǒng one type

When "一" is followed by a syllable in the fourth, it changes into the second tone.

  • 一定 yídìng surely, certainly

  • 一块 yíkuài one piece

When "一" is used alone or in a number, its tone doesn't change

  • 第一 dì yī first

  • 一二三 yī èr sān one, two , three

  • 星期一 xīngqí yī  Monday

  • 十一 shíyī eleven

  • 百分之一 bǎi fēn zhī yī one percent
